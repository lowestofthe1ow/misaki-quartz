**I. Distance and midpoint.** Do as instructed.

1. What would be the midpoint of a line segment with endpoints at $(0,1)$ and $(5,7)$?
2. If a line has a midpoint at $(2,5)$, and the endpoints are $(0,0)$ and $(4,y)$, what is the value of $y$?
3. Find the distance between the points $(1, 2)$ and $(4, 6)$.
4. Find the midpoint of the line segment with endpoints $(2, 3)$ and $(6, 7)$.
5. Find the distance between the points $(-2, -3)$ and $(4, 1)$.

**II. Slope.** Do as instructed.

6. Find the slope of the line passing through the points $(3, 4)$ and $(7, 10)$.
7. Find the slope of the line passing through the points $(-1, -2)$ and $(5, 6)$.
8. Determine if the lines passing through the points $(1, 2)$ and $(3, 4)$, and $(2, 3)$ and $(4, 5)$ are parallel.
9. Find the slope of the line perpendicular to the line passing through the points $(0, 0)$ and $(4, 8)$.
10. Find the slope of the line passing through the points $(-3, 5)$ and $(2, -1)$, and determine if it is perpendicular to the line passing through $(1, -1)$ and $(-2, 4)$.

**III. The equation of a line.** Do as instructed.

11. Find the equation of the line passing through the points $(3, -5)$ and $(-2, 4)$.
12. Find the equation of the line with slope $-\displaystyle \frac{3}{4}$ that passes through the point $(6, 2)$.
13. Find the equation of the line perpendicular to the line passing through the points $(1, 2)$ and $(4, -1)$, and passing through the point $(-3, 5)$.
14. Find the equation of the line parallel to the line passing through the points $(-1, 2)$ and $(2, -3)$ that passes through the point $(5, -4)$.
15. Find the equation of the line that passes through the point $(4, -2)$ and intersects the $y$-axis at $(0, 6)$.

**IV. Conic sections.** Do as instructed. Write all equations in *standard form*.

*Circle*

16. Find the equation of the circle with center $(2, -3)$ and radius $5$.
17. Find the center and radius of the circle with the equation $x^2 + y^2 - 6x + 8y - 15 = 0$.
18. Determine if the point $(4, -2)$ lies inside, outside, or on the circle with equation $(x - 3)^2 + (y + 1)^2 = 25$.
19. Find the equation of the circle with a center at $(0, 0)$ and passing through the point $(3, 4)$.
20. Find the center and radius of the circle with the equation $x^2 + y^2 - 10x - 6y + 9 = 0$.

*Parabola*

21. Find the equation of the parabola with vertex $(2, -3)$ and focus at $(2, 1)$.
22. Write the equation of the parabola with vertex $(0, 0)$ and directrix $y = -4$.
23. Find the vertex and axis of symmetry of the parabola given by the equation $y = 2x^2 - 4x + 3$.
24. Determine the equation of the parabola with vertex $(-1, 2)$ and passing through the point $(1, 6)$.
25. Find the equation of the parabola with focus at $(0, 3)$ and directrix $y = -3$.

*Ellipse*

26. Find the equation of the ellipse with center at $(0, 0)$, horizontal axis length 6, and vertical axis length 4.
27. Find the foci and the equation of the ellipse with equation $\displaystyle \frac{x^2}{9} + \displaystyle \frac{y^2}{16} = 1$.
28. Write the equation of an ellipse with a center at $(3, -2)$, horizontal axis length 8, and vertical axis length 6.
29. Find the center, foci, and equation of the ellipse with foci at $(0, \pm 5)$ and a major axis length of 12.
30. Determine the equation of the ellipse with a center at $(4, 1)$, major axis length 10, and minor axis length 6.

*Hyperbola*

31. Find the equation of the hyperbola with center at $(0, 0)$, transverse axis length 6, and conjugate axis length 4.
32. Write the equation of the hyperbola with foci at $(\pm 5, 0)$ and vertices at $(\pm 3, 0)$.
33. Find the center, foci, and equation of the hyperbola with center at $(2, -1)$, transverse axis length 10, and conjugate axis length 6.

**V. Systems of nonlinear equations.** Solve the following systems for $x$ and $y$.

34. $$\begin{align*}x^2 + y^2 &= 25 \\ y &= x + 3 \end{align*}$$
35. $$\begin{align*}x^2 + y^2 &= 9 \\ x - y &= 2 \end{align*}$$
36. $$\begin{align*}x^2 + y^2 &= 16 \\ x + y &= 4 \end{align*}$$
37. $$\begin{align*}x^2 - y^2 &= 7 \\ x + y &= 3 \end{align*}$$
38. $$\begin{align*}x^2 + y^2 &= 10 \\ 2x + y &= 4 \end{align*}$$

**VI. Sequences and series.** Do as instructed.

39. Find the 10th term of the arithmetic sequence where the first term is 5 and the common difference is 3.
40. Find the sum of the first 15 terms of the arithmetic sequence where the first term is 3 and the common difference is 5.
41. Find the sum of the first 20 terms of the arithmetic series where the first term is 7 and the common difference is 4.
42. Find the sum of the infinite geometric series with the first term 6 and common ratio $\displaystyle \frac{1}{2}$.
43. Find the sum of the first 15 terms of the geometric series where the first term is 3 and the common ratio is $-2$.

**VII. Summations.**

Find a closed-form expression for the following summations.

44. $$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k(k+1)$$
45. $$\sum_{k=1}^{n} k^3$$
46. $$\sum_{k=1}^{n} (5k^2 + 4)$$

Evaluate the following sums.

47. $$\sum_{k=1}^{7} (40-k^2)$$
48. $$\sum_{k=1}^{5} (4k^2+4)$$

**VIII. Mathematical induction.** Prove the following by induction, for all integers $n \geq 1$.

49. Show that the number $n^2 + n$ is divisible by 2.
50. Show that the expression $5^n - 1$ is divisible by 4.
51. Show that $2 + 5 + 8 + \dots + (3n - 1) = \displaystyle \frac{n(3n+1)}{2}$.
52. Show that $\displaystyle \sum_{m=1}^n (3m^2 + m) = n(n+1)^2$.

**IX. Binomial theorem.** Do as instructed.

53. Expand the binomial expression $(x + 2)^5$ using the binomial theorem.
54. Find the 4th term in the expansion of $(3x - 2y)^6$.
55. Using the binomial theorem, expand and simplify $(1 + 2x)^7$ up to the $x^3$ term.
