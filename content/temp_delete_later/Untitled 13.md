##### Problem 1.

Let $x$ be the distance Reden has traveled eastward from the origin and $x$ be the distance Neil has traveled southward from the origin. Assume that they started at the same time $t = 0$. Then:

$$
\begin{align*}
	\frac{\text{dx}}{\text{dt}} = 60 \text{ cm/s} &&
	\frac{\text{dy}}{\text{dt}} = 80 \text{ cm/s} 
\end{align*}
$$

Let $z$ be the distance between Reden and Neil. Then:

$$
\begin{align*}
	z^2 &= x^2 + y^2 \\
	\frac{\text{d}}{\text{dt}} (z^2) &= \frac{\text{d}}{\text{dt}} (x^2) + \frac{\text{d}}{\text{dt}} (y^2) \\
	2z \frac{\text{dz}}{\text{dt}} &= 2x \frac{\text{dx}}{\text{dt}} + 2y \frac{\text{dy}}{\text{dt}}
\end{align*}
$$

At the time $t = 2$ seconds, Reden has traveled $60(2) = 120$ cm eastward while Neil has traveled $80(2) = 160$ cm southward. Then the distance between them at $t = 2$ is given by (taking only the positive root):

$$
\begin{align*}
	z^2 &= x^2 + y^2 \\
	z^2 &= 120^2 + 160^2 \\
	z &= 200
\end{align*}
$$

We use these values to solve for $\displaystyle \frac{\text{dz}}{\text{dt}}$ at $t = 2$:

$$
\begin{align*}
	2(200) \frac{\text{dz}}{\text{dt}} &= 2(120) \frac{\text{dx}}{\text{dt}} + 2(160) \frac{\text{dy}}{\text{dt}} \\
	\frac{\text{dz}}{\text{dt}} &= \frac{2(120) (60) + 2(160) (80)}{2(200) } \\
	&= \frac{40000}{400} \\
	&= 100 \text{ cm/s}
\end{align*}
$$
